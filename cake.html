Welcome to the Cake Page!
choose one of this options as your home made cake

Moroccan Orange Cake

A moist and tender cake that is flavored with fresh orange juice and zest.
It's a popular dessert in Moroccan cuisine and is often served with a dusting of powdered
sugar or a drizzle of orange glaze.

Moroccan Basbousa

a traditional Moroccan dessert made with semolina flour, coconut, sugar, and yogurt.
The mixture is baked until golden brown and then soaked in a sweet syrup flavored with rose water
or orange blossom water.

Moroccan Lemon Cake

A crunchy and slightly sweet cookie that is studded with almonds and sesame seeds.
It is often eaten as a snack or served with tea.

Moroccan Almond Snake Pastry

A dense and chewy cookie made from ground almonds, sugar, and eggs. It comes in a variety of flavors, such as coconut, chocolate, and lemon.

Moroccan Baklava

baklava is a sweet and rich pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, butter, and a filling of chopped nuts such as almonds, pistachios, and walnuts, as well as sweetened with honey or sugar syrup.

Moroccan Jelly Cake

typically consists of a light and fluffy sponge cake that is layered with fruit-flavored jelly, whipped cream, and fresh fruit.

Moroccan Kounafa

is a traditional Moroccan pastry
made with a base of shredded phyllo dough that is then filled with sweetened cheese
or cream, soaked in syrup flavored with rose water or orange blossom water.

Moroccan Chocolate Cake

Chocolate cake is a classic dessert that is loved all over the world, including in Morocco.
It's typically made with a moist and rich chocolate sponge cake that is layered with frosting
or ganache and then decorated with chocolate shavings, fresh berries,
or other toppings.

Moroccan Mille feuille

Mille feuille, also known as "thousand leaves" in French, is a layered pastry
that consists of multiple layers of flaky puff pastry
that are alternated with layers of pastry cream,
whipped cream, or fruit.
