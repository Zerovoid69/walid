Our Food Culture in Morocco

Moroccan cuisine is a vibrant blend of flavors and ingredients influenced by the country's geography, history, and diverse cultural heritage. With a mix of Berber, Arab, and Sub-Saharan African influences, Moroccan food is known for its use of spices, slow-cooked stews, and iconic dishes like couscous and tagine.

Saharian cuisine

is a reflection of the desert environment and nomadic lifestyle of the region. Characterized by simple ingredients and traditional cooking methods, Saharan food includes dishes like couscous with dates, lamb or camel meat, and flatbread baked in underground ovens.

Arabian cuisine

with many of the country's most famous dishes featuring a range of spices and flavors from the Arab world. From savory pies like b'stilla to hearty stews like shakshuka, Arab cuisine has left an indelible mark on Moroccan food culture.

Berbers cuisine

is a fundamental part of Moroccan culinary traditions. As the indigenous people of the region, the Berbers have developed a unique food culture based on locally grown grains, fruits, and vegetables. Berber dishes like couscous and tagine are renowned for their rich flavors and distinctive use of spices.
