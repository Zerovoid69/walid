Welcome to the Cookie Page!
choose one of this options as your home made cookies

Moroccan Krichlate

The dough is shaped into small balls and then baked until golden brown and crispy.
Krichlate has a delicate and crunchy texture and a sweet and nutty flavor
that is often enjoyed with tea or coffee.

Moroccan Fekkas

made with a blend of flour, sugar, eggs, and butter, and then flavored with ingredients such as orange blossom water, sesame seeds, anise, and cinnamon.

Moroccan Choco Cookies

made with a combination of flour, cocoa powder, sugar, butter, and eggs, and then flavored with vanilla extract or other spices such as cinnamon.

Moroccan Ghriba

made with almond flour, sugar, egg whites, and a variety of flavorings such as sesame seeds, coconut, or orange blossom water. Ghriba cookies have a soft and chewy texture with a crispy exterior.

Moroccan Chabakiya

prepared during the month of Ramadan. It is made by mixing flour, semolina, and spices like anise, sesame seeds, and cinnamon,
and then kneading the mixture into a dough.
The dough is then rolled out into strips and cut into diamond shapes.

Moroccan Classic Cookies

They are usually made with a combination of flour, sugar, butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. The dough is then shaped into small balls, flattened, and baked until golden brown.
